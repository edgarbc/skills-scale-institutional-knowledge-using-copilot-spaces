# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers shape user experiences so product outcomes are usable, accessible, and aligned to customer needs. They translate product goals into interaction flows and interface decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Turn problem statements into user journeys and wireframes
- Define interaction patterns and usability considerations
- Partner with Developers during implementation to preserve intent
- Participate in design reviews and acceptance discussions

### Goals
- Reduce usability friction and rework
- Improve task completion, satisfaction, and adoption
- Keep design intent clear from planning through release

### Typical Communication
- Discovery and design workshops with PdM/PM
- Wireframe walkthroughs with Developers and QA
- Design review notes and annotated prototypes

---

## Data Analysts

### Role Summary
Data Analysts enable data-informed decisions by defining metrics, building reporting views, and interpreting performance signals across the lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define metric definitions with Product Managers
- Build or maintain dashboards tied to project outcomes
- Analyze usage/performance trends and highlight risks
- Support release and retrospective reviews with evidence

### Goals
- Ensure teams align on metric definitions and targets
- Improve decision quality with timely analysis
- Surface leading indicators early enough to act

### Typical Communication
- KPI alignment syncs with Product Managers
- Dashboard and trend reviews with PM/PdM/Developers
- Metric commentary in release and retrospective updates

---

## Customer Success Managers

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers represent customer outcomes after delivery, bringing adoption and support insights back into planning and prioritization.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and triage customer feedback and recurring pain points
- Escalate high-impact customer issues to PM/PdM
- Share onboarding/adoption insights with delivery teams
- Help prioritize follow-up improvements based on customer impact

### Goals
- Increase adoption and customer satisfaction
- Reduce time to resolve customer-impacting issues
- Keep product priorities connected to customer reality

### Typical Communication
- Feedback summaries and escalation notes to PM/PdM
- Customer readiness updates before and after release
- Trend summaries for planning and retrospective sessions

## Interaction & Hand-offs

### UX Designer interactions
- **With Product Managers:** consumes problem statements, constraints, and success metrics; produces wireframes/prototypes and interaction notes; PdM signs off on scope/intent before engineering implementation.
- **With Project Managers:** aligns design milestones with delivery timeline and dependency planning; PM confirms schedule impact and review cadence.
- **With Developers/QA:** hands off annotated wireframes and acceptance criteria clarifications; Developers confirm technical feasibility and QA confirms testability before implementation is marked ready.

### Data Analyst interactions
- **With Product Managers:** co-defines KPI and metric definitions; produces dashboard links and analysis summaries; PdM approves metric baselines and target thresholds.
- **With Project Managers:** shares risk/forecast indicators for status reporting; PM decides escalation path when trends indicate delivery risk.
- **With Developers/QA:** consumes instrumentation notes and release context; provides metric validation inputs and post-release signal reviews to support quality and iteration decisions.

### Customer Success Manager interactions
- **With Product Managers:** provides feedback triage summaries, top customer pain points, and adoption themes; PdM approves prioritization trade-offs based on impact.
- **With Project Managers:** escalates operational/customer risks and communication needs; PM confirms response owners and stakeholder messaging.
- **With Developers/QA:** shares reproducible customer scenarios and impact context; engineering/QA incorporate into bug triage, validation, and release-readiness checks.

### Shared hand-off artifacts
- **Produced/consumed artifacts:** PRDs/feature briefs, wireframes/prototypes, acceptance criteria, test plan notes, dashboard links, customer feedback summaries, decision logs.
- **Decision/approval points:** PdM approves product intent/priority, PM approves delivery plan and escalation ownership, Developers/QA approve implementation/test readiness, Stakeholders/Sponsor approve major scope or release decisions.
